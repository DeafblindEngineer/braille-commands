### Visio Braille VarioPro 80 with Status Module

Braille Commands for Linux assistive-technology stack:

* Orca screen reader program in graphical-mode
* Speakup a kernel-based screen reader for the Linux console in text-mode
* BRLTTY daemon to power the VarioPro 80 with Status Module

### Orientation of VarioPro 80 with Status Module

The VarioPro 80 with the Status Module is an 84-cell refreshable braille display, with routing keys, six display keys, four command keys, and four rollbars.

#### The Top Face

The top section of the braille display is divided into two sections: main and status. The main section of the display has a consists of 80 refreshable cells with a cursor routing key above each cell. There are two space bars below the display. There are six display keys, three to the left of the refreshable braille display, and three to the right. On the left, starting from the back, are D1, D2 and D3, which is the one to the front of the display. On the right side, are D4, D5 and D6.

The status section of the braille display has four refreshable cells with a routing key above each cell. Above the refreshable cells are four command keys: C1, C2, C3, C4.

#### Front Edge

On the front edge of the display are four rollbars. From left to right, the rollbars are as follows:

* R1 (up, press, down)
* R2 (up, press, down)
* R3 (up, press, down)
* R4 (up, press, down)

The display keys, command keys, and rollbars can be used to perform navigation commands:
